Recent news

The Washington Post earned two Pulitzer Prizes this year with significant contributions from designers, including Social Security wait times and “He’s dying. She’s pregnant.” The Post was also recognized with three of the top honors from the Society for News Design: Best in Show for coverage of the Middle East conflict, plus World’s Best Designer (Tyler Remmel) and World’s Best Emerging Designer (Hailey Haymond). In the announcement, SND secretary Rebecca Pazos said: “The work (The Post) produced in the past few years will be held up as the gold standard for years to come, a legacy that will not be easily forgotten.”

Be sure to check out their related interviews. Remmel on the power of visual storytelling (May 9): “My favorite designs are those that are seen but not heard, that amplify the stories but don’t distract from them.” Haymond on emotion and interactivity (May 27): “Design has to make readers want to stay, and once they do, they’re more open to sitting with complexity.”


About me

Former deputy director and most recently department head of news design at The Washington Post. I joined The Post during a significant period of modernization and growth. As a manager for 15-plus years, I forged key partnerships, improved systems and scaled repeatable formats. Designers learned to code premium immersive presentations and shaped the defining journalism of the era, contributing to 14 Pulitzer winners and 17 finalists over the past decade (including staff Pulitzers for National Reporting on American Icon in 2024 and Public Service for January 6 coverage in 2022). Previously at the Boston Globe and San Diego Union-Tribune.

Awards & volunteering

Organized and ran Society for News Design digital competition from 2019-2024, judged in 2005 and 2016, and served as team captain for juding teams in 2025 and 2026. Recognized by SND with multiple silver medals and portfolio awards, including World's Best Digital (2020, 2025), Best in Show (2020, 2024, 2025) and World's Best Newspaper Finalist (2013-15, 2020, 2022). Five-time SND workshop speaker (2002, 2005, 2015, 2025, 2026). Also awarded a Gold Medal for Terror on Repeat by the Society of Publication Designers in 2024 and mentored as part of SPD's Coffee + Convo program in 2025.

Education & background

Graduate of journalism school at Southern Illinois University Carbondale. Became a baseball fan growing up just outside St. Louis, and my dream job since the 8th grade was to be a sports writer.
