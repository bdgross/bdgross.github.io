Projects

Long-form, exclusive and investigative storytelling, data-driven visuals, immersive explainers. Elevated through craft, planning and deep collaboration. I’ve worked on and overseen some of The Post’s most ambitious visual journalism—projects that regularly earned industry recognition for design excellence and innovation. Early in the process, I shape ideas, assess scope, and align creative vision with realistic timelines and resources. When the stakes or complexity call for it, I step in directly to make hands-on contributions.


A rare look at the devastation caused by AR-15 shootings →

Designed a 38-page tabloid-size print special section for Sunday subscribers; personally adapted the digital story for print given the potentially traumatizing material. Included with the entry that won the Pulitzer Prize for National Reporting, recognized as Best in Show by the Society for News Design and awarded a Gold Medal from the Society of Publication Designers. (The Washington Post, December 23, 2023)

Before during after the attack →

Design editor for three-part digital enterprise package; contributed early concepts on structure, use of color and overall aesthetic, including the navigation device and wording. Included with the entry that won the Pulitzer Prize for Public Service. (The Washington Post, October 31, 2021)

Every team USA gold, silver and bronze medal →

Design editor for collection or American medal winners that updated throughout the games and included filtering options. Pitched the idea, refined the UX and typography through early mockups, manually updated results and republished daily. Recognized with Award of Excellence by the Society for News Design. (The Washington Post, Aug. 10, 2024)

Willie Mays timeline and career highlights →

Designer, researcher and writer for digital presentation prepared in advance. Contacted the Topps company for permission to use the baseball cards. Researched, wrote and designed both digital and print presentations. Text was also used as a carousel for social. (The Washington Post, June 19, 2024)

Worth the Wait

Designed, planned and edited a 128-page commemorative book on deadline for the Stanley Cup championship won by the Washington Capitals. Recognized with Silver Medal from the Society for News Design. (Triumph Books, July 2018)

Fight to the Finish

Designed, planned and edited a 128-page commemorative book on deadline for the World Series championship won by the Washington Nationals. Updated to included additional photos from the parade celebration as a 142-page hard cover version. (Triumph Books, December 2019)

The Mueller Report Illustrated →

Design editor for six-chapter digital package, involved from earliest stages of story boarding the “graphic novel” concept. Designed cover for a 208-page book in collaboration with Scribner. Recognized as Best in Show from the Society for News Design, 2020. (The Washington Post, November 2019)

Too many men →

Design editor for this digital project, shaping the overall presentation, experimental side-scroll structure and art direction from early in the process. Recognized with digital bronze medal from the Society for News Design. (The Washington Post, April 18, 2018)

Drowing in garbage →

Designer for an exclusive in-depth visuals project, collaborating with the Director of Photography and freelancer Kadir van Lohuizen, shaping the UX and navigation for the digital presentation with an emphasis on mobile, creating an early Instagram carousel, and producing a print special section. Recognized with five awards from Society for News Design including excellence for print-digital combination and silver medal for photo series. (The Washington Post, Nov. 21, 2017)

The new Arctic frontier →

Designer for an exclusive in-depth visuals project, collaborating with the Director of Photography, freelancer Kadir van Lohuizen and a national security reporter, shaping the UX for the digital presentation with an emphasis on transitions for mobile and producing a print special section. Recognized with Silver Medal for photo series from the Society for News Design. (The Washington Post, November 21, 2018)

The year America unraveled →

Designer for 50th anniversary digital package and special print section. Commissioned 81-year-old graphic artist Lance Wyman, who designed the iconic logo for the 1968 Mexico Olympics, to create an updated version for use as animated topper, print cover and logo. Interviewed graphic designers Milton Glaser and George Lois fora story about their distinctive work from the era. (The Washington Post, May 28, 2018)

Cycling’s road forward →

Designer on this experimental long-form story that integrated narrative, graphics, photo and video at The Post for the first time, collaborating with the design director and a developer on the aesthetic, navigation, pacing and grid. A special Word Press template for enterprise stories was based on the layer, and later evolved into several iterations of custom presentation formats. Recognized with SND Award of Excellence for combination print and digital design. (The Washington Post, February 27, 2013)

Where the game is always on →

Design editor for a project on cities with all four major professional sports. Provided the data and analysis for the graphics and collaborated with the sports department to build a package that included blurbs from writers around the country. (The Washington Post, April 14, 2015)

How many ballparks have you visited? →

Design editor for an interactive checklist featuring the 30 major-league baseball ballparks, conceived as part travel guide and part ranking in collaboration with the sports department. Related: How Thomas Boswell ranked all 30 MLB ballparks. (The Washington Post, July 11, 2017)
