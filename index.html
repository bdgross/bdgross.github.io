My name is Brian Gross

I’m a designer, editor and leader who on a daily basis bridges the gap between reporters and creatives. I lead a team of designers, art directors and front-end developers who build bespoke digital storytelling experiences at The Washington Post. My speciality is integrated storytelling, prioritzing the use of visuals including illustration to deeply engage audiences.

Some projects I’ve edited over the years

Since I came to The Post as a senior designer and editor in 2014, I’ve gotten a chance to be a part of dozens of The Post’s most exciting and innovative projects including Pulitzer Prize winning stories, months-long investigations and quick turnaround projects. My role varies: sometimes I’m working with the editors and designers to shape the story, other times refining the UX or presentation for the smallest screens. My job is to make sure that we overcome any challenges, and that the stories publish on time across all platforms.
